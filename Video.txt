0:48 – 1:25 · PIPELINE (conceptual → live)             (≈95 words)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VISUAL: Pipeline diagram animates — four boxes light up in
        sequence as you narrate. Then cut to live UI: Live tab
        streaming, rollouts populating, keyframes filling the
        gallery.

VO: "Four specialized Opus 4.7 agents work in parallel.
     A **planner** turns 'evaluate this policy' into a test
     matrix. A **rollout worker** runs the simulation. A swarm
     of **judge workers** — one per failed rollout — watches
     the video at 2576-pixel resolution, names the earliest
     failure frame, and points at the exact pixel where things
     went wrong. A **reporter** clusters every finding in a
     1-million-token context window and writes the report.
     And when there's nothing to point at — say the gripper
     closed on empty air — the judge returns `point: null` and
     abstains. No fake red dot. No hallucinated evidence."

VO: "Here's the part nobody else does. Half the rollouts come

     a human labels a sample of those. That's ground truth.
     The other half come from a real pretrained policy under
     stress. The same judge runs on both.
     The judge's precision against the human labels becomes a
     **trust chip** on every deployment finding.
     PolicyGrader doesn't just grade the robot.
     **It grades the grader.**"

VISUAL: Standalone scene (JudgeChallengesScene), 12 s, 4 stages:

        2) cycling cal_06/07/08 missed_approach keyframes whose
           dots landed at arbitrary pixels (real pre-fix output

        3) cal_09.mp4 plays clean, green `point = null` chip
           lands ("judge abstains")
        4) cal_04 keyframe + ASCII sim-telemetry table from
           cal_04.telemetry.json; the contact column flipping
           ✓ → − between f41 and f52 is the slip moment

VO: "One challenge worth flagging. Opus 4.7 returns pixel
     coordinates — we use that to mark exactly where each
     failure happens. But forcing it to point on every
     rollout was wrong: when the arm misses the cube
     entirely, there's nothing to point at and the dot
     lands anywhere. Making `point = null` a first-class
     answer fixed it. And where vision alone was still
     ambiguous, we paste simulator telemetry — gripper,
     contact flag, cube height — alongside the frames.
     Pixels grounded by physics."
